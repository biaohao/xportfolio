Risk-Return Profile

IEF: Conservative Efficiency
Nearly identical CAGR at half the volatility. Calmar 0.153 vs 0.076 — earns the same total return per unit of peak drawdown. Preferred in risk-managed and TAA portfolios.
TLT: High-Beta Duration Play
~17yr duration amplifies every rate move ~3×. In crises (2008, 2011, 2020) TLT surges 18–34%. In rate-hiking cycles (2009, 2022) it can lose 22–31% in a single year.
Correlation = 0.918 — Both ETFs track the same Treasury curve and move directionally together ~92% of the time. TLT is effectively a leveraged version of IEF: higher upside capture, proportionally higher downside.

Duration Mechanics

IEF — ~7.5yr Modified Duration
100bp parallel rate rise → approx. −7.5% price loss. Recoverable within 1–2 years of coupon income (~4–5%), so a moderate rate rise need not produce a negative total return.
TLT — ~16–18yr Modified Duration
The same 100bp rise wipes ~−17% from price. In 2022 the Fed hiked +425bp in 12 months — TLT's worst year on record (−31%), erasing over a decade of coupon income in one year.
