Key Discrepancies Explained

MetricReason for gap
Vol: 15.5% vs ~17% (B&H) Faber uses GFD total-return indices for all 5 assets from 1973; our EAFE/GSCI/VNQ are ETF adjusted close. ETF adjusted-close series can have slightly different volatility scaling than index total-return series.
Timing Vol: 7.9% vs ~6.8% Same data-source difference. Our spliced series for EAFE/GSCI/VNQ uses ETF prices which can have slightly higher day-to-day noise than the GFD index series.
% Invested: 66% vs ~70% Pre-2001 periods where EAFE/GSCI/VNQ are unavailable in our dataset count as "cash", pulling the average down. Within the 2006–2012 window all 5 ETFs exist, so the gap is small (3.8pp).
2010 Timing: +3% vs ~+11% Largest annual discrepancy. Likely reflects different construction of the GSCI commodity index — GFD's series and the GSG ETF diverged meaningfully in 2010 due to roll methodology differences in the S&P GSCI.
