Key Takeaways

1
50/50 Timing is the best all-weather portfolio. Over 50 years it delivers 11.46% CAGR with only 8.57% volatility — a Sharpe of 0.818. Max drawdown is −16.15% vs −49% for pure SP500 B&H. In the modern era (2007–2023) it achieves Sharpe 1.064 with a max-DD of just −9.61%.
2
Timing dramatically improves all three portfolios. For 50/50: +2.2 pp CAGR, vol cut from 10.7% to 8.6%, max-DD halved from −33% to −16%. For SP500: +1.8 pp CAGR, vol cut 28%, max-DD cut 61%. For Gold: +3.1 pp CAGR, max-DD cut 62%.
3
Gold timing is more powerful than gold B&H. Gold's SMA-10 timing cuts its worst year from −27.5% to −10.3% and its max-DD from −62% to −24%. In the full period, Gold Timing CAGR is 9.99% — nearly matching SP500 B&H (10.45%) with a much better worst-year profile.
4
50/50 B&H alone already beats SP500 on risk metrics. Even without timing, the 50/50 mix reduces max-DD from −49% to −33% and vol from 12.7% to 10.7% — due to gold's near-zero correlation with stocks. The CAGR cost is only −1.2 pp/yr.
5
The timing rule spent only 65% of time invested. The other 35% earns T-bill cash. This "dry powder" effect also dampened returns in strong bull markets (e.g. 2020: 50/50 Timing +13.3% vs B&H +22.9%) — the cost of insurance. Investors who can stay disciplined benefit most.
