Key Observations

B&H dominates in the early hyper-growth years (2010–2013, 2017, 2020–2021) when Bitcoin delivered triple- and quadruple-digit annual returns — SMA timing was partially invested and captured only a fraction.

Timing shines in crash protection: the 2018 and 2022 bear markets saw B&H lose ~73% and ~65% respectively. The SMA-10 signal exited near the top of both cycles, limiting drawdowns to roughly half the B&H figure.

ETF era (2014–2026): Timing beats B&H on CAGR (+67% vs +54%), Sharpe (1.12 vs 0.94), and max drawdown (−60% vs −76%) — the only period where the strategy simultaneously lifts return and cuts risk.

Volatility context: BTC vol (71–178% annualised) is 5–12× equity vol, so even a 5% allocation in a diversified portfolio meaningfully shifts the risk budget.

6 · Data & Methodology Notes

Price sources: CoinMetrics open data (GitHub, daily PriceUSD) for 2010-07-18 – 2014-09-16; Yahoo Finance BTC-USD (adjusted close) from 2014-09-17 onward. The two series agree to within ~0.7% RMSE at month-end in the overlap. Both are resampled to month-end (last trade of the month) before splicing.

Splice methodology: ETF series takes precedence from its first observation. The long-history series is scaled so that its value at the splice month equals the ETF's first price, preserving the true return in the splice month.

Timing rule: signal = 1 (invested) when prior month-end price > 10-month SMA, else 0 (cash at T-bill rate). Signal is computed on end-of-month prices and applied to the following month's return to avoid look-ahead bias. Cash return = BIL ETF (FRED TB3MS pre-2007). First 10 months of each period are consumed by SMA warm-up and excluded.

Portfolios: monthly rebalanced to equal weight; timing applied independently to each leg. No transaction costs, taxes, or bid-ask spreads modelled.
