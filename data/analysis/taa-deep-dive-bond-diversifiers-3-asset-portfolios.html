TAA Deep Dive: Bond Diversifiers & 3-Asset Portfolios

Monthly SMA-10 timing · Equal-weight · No look-ahead bias · Log-space wealth accumulation (overflow-safe) · Data through June 2026
Key findings
  • IEF overflow bug fixed — prior 1962+ SP500 Timing rows showed inf due to float64 overflow in 64-year cumprod(); log-space accumulation resolves this. SP500 Timing (1962–2026): CAGR 9.85%, Sharpe 1.006.
  • Adding Gold as a third asset sharply reduces drawdowns vs a 2-asset bond+equity mix, at the cost of a modest CAGR reduction (~0.5–1 pp), because gold and bonds diversify different crises: gold handles inflationary shocks (1970s, 2022), bonds handle deflationary/recessionary shocks (2001–2002, 2008).
  • SP500+Gold+IEF Timing is the most efficient 3-asset strategy overall: Sharpe 1.253–1.321 across all windows, max-DD contained to −8.5% to −10.9%.
  • SP500+Gold+TLT Timing delivers slightly higher CAGR in the Faber era (9.72% vs 9.06%) but TLT's duration risk bleeds through in drawdown — max-DD −11.7% vs −10.9% for the IEF version.
  • 2022 stress test: SP500+Gold+IEF Timing lost −7.6%, SP500+Gold+TLT Timing −8.2%, vs SP500 alone −18.2% and 60/40 (SP500+IEF B&H) −16.3%.
  • The 3-asset Timing portfolios are invested only ~65–72% of the time — holding 28–35% cash on average costs CAGR but buys the low-drawdown profile.

6 · Interpretation & Practical Conclusions

Question Answer (from data)
IEF vs TLT as diversifier?IEF wins on risk-adjusted basis in every window. TLT delivers higher raw returns in the 1977–2012 bond bull, but at 2× the volatility. Post-2012 TLT is actively harmful: B&H CAGR 3.0% with −47.6% max-DD.
Does adding Gold to SP+IEF help?Modestly in B&H (Sharpe 1.129 vs 1.017 over 1962–2026). Significantly in Timing: the 3-asset timing cuts max-DD by −3.6 pp vs the 2-asset version (−10.9% vs −14.4%), while lifting Sharpe 1.253 vs 1.174.
Does Gold replace or complement bonds?Complement. Gold and IEF are uncorrelated (corr ≈ 0.0 to 0.1), but they hedge different crises. Holding both provides "all-weather" protection: Gold for inflationary shocks (1970s, 2022), IEF for deflationary recessions (2001–02, 2008).
Does timing help the 3-asset combo?Yes, but mainly on the risk side. Timing Sharpe 1.253 vs B&H 1.129 (full period), max-DD cut from −22.2% to −10.9%. CAGR cost: ~0.75 pp (8.34% vs 9.09%) from the cash drag of being only 68% invested.
Best crisis protection?3-asset Timing (SP+Gold+IEF): 2008 only −7.6% (vs SP500 −36.8%), 2022 only −7.6% (vs SP500 −18.2%). 2-asset SP+IEF B&H: 2022 −16.3%. Adding Gold as a third leg insulates against inflationary bear markets.
Real-world implementation?Monthly rebalance on last trading day. For each asset: if price > 10-month SMA → hold, else → T-bills. ETFs: SPY/VOO + GLD/IAU + IEF. Execution: ~3–4 trades/year average. Transaction costs are small at monthly frequency.
