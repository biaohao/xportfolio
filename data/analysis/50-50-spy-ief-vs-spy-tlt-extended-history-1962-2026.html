Key Takeaways from Extended History

Timing earns its keep over 64 years
Across the full spliced history, IEF Timing beats IEF B&H on CAGR (+8.72% vs +8.40%), Sharpe (0.753 vs 0.543) and Ulcer (2.46 vs 4.81). This is the opposite of the short ETF window — because the longer history includes multiple inflationary bear markets where timing adds genuine alpha.
Ulcer Index is the key differentiator
IEF Timing's Ulcer Index of 2.46% vs 4.81% for B&H means investors spent far less time, and far shallower, underwater. UPI of 1.919 vs 0.916 — timing generates more than twice the return per unit of sustained drawdown stress over the full 64-year period.
Only 6 negative years with timing vs 14 B&H
Over 65 calendar years, IEF Timing posted negative returns in only 6 years (2015, 2018, 2022 and three minor ones). IEF B&H had 14 negative years. For a real investor tracking annual statements, this is psychologically enormous.
The 2000s decade is the clearest case
SPY B&H returned −1.02%/yr (a lost decade). IEF B&H managed only +3.33%/yr with a −23% drawdown. IEF Timing delivered +6.54%/yr with only a −5.9% max drawdown — exiting equities during both the dot-com bust and the GFC before the worst damage.
Why the short window (2007–2026) flatters B&H and penalises timing. The ETF-only window starts just before the GFC (where timing shines) but is dominated by the longest bull market in history (2009–2021), where the SMA timing model repeatedly rotates into cash and misses gains. The 64-year view corrects for this recency bias — over full cycles, including high-inflation regimes, timing adds genuine value on both an absolute and risk-adjusted basis.
