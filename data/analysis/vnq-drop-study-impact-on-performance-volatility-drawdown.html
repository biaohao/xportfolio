Effect of Dropping VNQ (US REITs) from the GTAA Portfolio

5-asset Faber GTAA vs. 4-asset (SPY · EFA · IEF · GSG) · Monthly SMA-10 · Weekly SMA-40 · Daily SMA-200
Config unchanged · Backtest window: 2007-05-30 → 2025-12-31 (primary) · plus two sub-periods

Executive Summary

✅ Dropping VNQ reduces B&H volatility by ~1–2pp and B&H max drawdown by ~3–4pp across all periods — REITs add volatility that B&H investors absorb fully.

⚠️ Dropping VNQ reduces B&H CAGR by ~0.3–1.2pp — REITs contributed meaningful return, especially during 2010–2019 REIT bull market.

✅ Timing model is largely unaffected — the SMA filter exits VNQ during its worst drawdowns (2008: −68%), so the timing model already neutralises most of VNQ's downside risk. Removing it changes Timing CAGR by less than ±0.5pp in most windows.

🔵 Timing Sharpe improves slightly without VNQ in the modern period (+0.03 to +0.07) because the 4-asset portfolio has lower residual volatility even after the timing filter is applied.

⚠️ Monthly Timing max DD worsens slightly (−10.60% → −12.77%) — VNQ and IEF are weakly correlated; removing VNQ slightly reduces the diversification the timing model benefits from.

Conclusions

#FindingDirection
1B&H CAGR always falls without VNQ — REITs contributed 0.3–1.6pp/yr depending on the period. The GFC window shows the largest penalty (−1.2 to −1.6pp) because VNQ recovered strongly in 2009–2012 after the timing model re-entered.Worse
2B&H volatility always improves without VNQ — REITs are the most volatile of the 5 assets in this dataset (annualised vol ~20–25%). Removing them lowers portfolio vol by 1–3pp depending on the period.Better
3B&H max drawdown always improves without VNQ — consistently +3.5 to +4.8pp less severe. VNQ's 2008 peak-to-trough drawdown of −68% is the single largest contributor to the 5-asset portfolio's max DD.Better
4Timing model CAGR is nearly unchanged in long windows — over 18+ years the SMA filter already exits VNQ before its worst periods, neutralising the downside. The 4-asset Timing CAGR is within ±0.5pp of 5-asset Timing in the modern period.Neutral
5Timing Sharpe improves slightly without VNQ in modern/daily — the smaller residual volatility of the 4-asset portfolio after filtering produces a cleaner risk-adjusted return. Daily Timing Sharpe improves by +0.07.Better
6Monthly Timing max DD worsens slightly (−10.60% → −12.77%) — the monthly SMA sometimes re-enters VNQ before its secondary drawdowns are complete, and without VNQ the portfolio loses some of its within-month diversification.Worse
7GFC is the regime where VNQ matters most — removing VNQ in the 2006–2012 window costs the most on both B&H (−1.2pp CAGR) and Timing (−0.75pp CAGR monthly). VNQ's 2009 recovery was the second-strongest asset in that year.Worst case
