Why not futures (GC=F)? Gold futures include a roll cost when the front-month contract expires and the position rolls to the next contract. In contango markets (usual for gold) this erodes returns. Over 2004–2026, GC=F returned 9.22× vs GLD's 8.48× — but GC=F incorrectly appears better because it reflects leverage from the notional futures value, not a fully-funded investment. GLD/IAU are the correct like-for-like comparison to buying and holding gold bullion.

Decision: Use GLD as the config ticker — it gives 48 more trading days of history (back to Nov 2004) and is the standard gold ETF used in academic replications of Faber's GTAA. The 0.15pp expense ratio difference is immaterial for backtesting purposes. IAU is the better live investment choice due to lower costs.

For the TAA backtest: The effective common start date when adding GLD to the 5-asset portfolio is 2007-05-30 (when BIL launches), which is later than GLD's 2004 start. Adding gold does not change the backtest start date — GLD simply has NaN for the 2.5 years before the common start, which the strategy engine already handles by renormalising weights.

5. How to Add Gold to the Portfolio

GLD is already downloaded and cached at data/raw/etf/GLD.csv. To include it in the backtest, add one line to config.yaml:

assets:
  SPY: "US Large Cap (S&P 500)"
  EFA: "Foreign Developed (MSCI EAFE)"
  IEF: "US 10-Year Bonds"
  GSG: "Commodities (GSCI)"
  VNQ: "US REITs (NAREIT)"
  GLD: "Gold"          ← add this line

Then run python3 main.py --backtest. No code changes needed — the strategy engine automatically rebalances to 1/6 equal weight for 6 assets.
