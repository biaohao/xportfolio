Key Observations

#Finding
1 SPY and Gold (GLD/IAU) are the top performers — both ~10.4–10.9% CAGR over their full histories. Gold's performance is particularly striking; it is the only non-equity asset to match US large-cap returns, driven by the post-2008 safe-haven demand and 2022–2025 rally (+63.7% in 2025 alone).
2 GSG (Commodities) is the worst performer — negative CAGR of −2.73% over 19.9 years with a catastrophic max drawdown of −89.6%. The GSCI is heavily energy-weighted (~60% oil); it collapsed in 2008, 2014–2016, and COVID 2020. This is the weakest asset in the Faber universe by a wide margin in the ETF era.
3 VNQ has the highest volatility (28.4%) — nearly 10pp above equities. Its max drawdown of −73% reflects the 2008 GFC where US commercial real estate was the epicentre of the crisis. Yet its 78.3% positive-year rate suggests it recovers strongly in bull markets.
4 IEF (bonds) has the best win rate after SPY at 76.0% positive years, low volatility (6.8%), and the only asset whose best year was 2008 (+17.9%) — the classic flight-to-safety behaviour that makes bonds a diversifier.
5 BIL has the highest Sharpe ratio (2.72) — trivially, because its vol is only 0.49%. This is not a meaningful comparison vs risky assets; it simply reflects that T-bills had near-zero drawdown. The Calmar of 1.73 is equally non-comparable.
6 Gold vs IAU — IAU marginally beats GLD on every metric (10.85% vs 10.42% CAGR, 0.657 vs 0.637 Sharpe) as expected from the 0.15pp lower expense ratio. Both are functionally identical for backtesting; use GLD for broadest history (Nov 2004 vs Jan 2005).
7 2008 was the worst year for equities and commodities simultaneously — SPY −36.8%, EFA −41.0%, VNQ −37.0%, GSG −45.8%. Only IEF (+17.9%) and BIL protected capital. This is exactly why Faber's timing model added such dramatic value in the GFC window.
