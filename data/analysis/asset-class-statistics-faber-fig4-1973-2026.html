CAGR = compound annual growth rate. Vol = annualised std dev of monthly returns. Sharpe = excess return over T-bills ÷ vol (dynamic T-bill rate; BIL Sharpe = 0 by construction). Max DD = maximum peak-to-trough decline on monthly prices. Ulcer Index = RMS of all below-peak deviations — captures both depth and duration of drawdowns (larger = more painful). $1 → = terminal value of $1 invested Jan 1973. DD Peak / Trough = calendar dates of the largest drawdown episode. Duration = months from peak to trough. Recovery = first month wealth returned to prior peak (IEF not yet recovered as of Jun 2026).
