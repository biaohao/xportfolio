Risk-Return Analysis

IEF: Consistent Risk-Adjusted Leader
Wins on Sharpe, Calmar, and Ulcer Index in both periods. Over 63 years IEF also edges TLT on terminal value ($40.47 vs $38.40) while running at half the volatility. The 2022 rate-hike cycle (−15.2% vs −31.2%) is the clearest demonstration of IEF's structural advantage in adverse rate environments.
TLT: High-Beta Duration, Context-Dependent
Wins on terminal return in the 2000–2026 window ($3.23 vs $3.07) — but at 2× the vol, 2× the max DD, and 2.6× the avg DD. Crisis years (2000, 2002, 2008, 2011, 2014, 2020) reward TLT with outsized gains; rate-hiking years (2009, 2013, 2022) deliver proportionally larger losses.
Correlation stays near 0.91 in both periods. Both ETFs track the same Treasury curve and move directionally together ~91% of the time. TLT is effectively a ~2.3× duration-leveraged version of IEF: larger upside capture in rallies, proportionally larger drawdowns in sell-offs. The leverage ratio is consistent across both study windows.
Avg Drawdown: the hidden risk measure. Max drawdown (IEF −23%, TLT −48%) captures only the worst single episode. Average drawdown tells you the typical lived experience: IEF sits on average only −3.1% below its prior peak over 63 years, vs TLT's −7.3%. In the 2000–2026 window both worsen (IEF −4.4%, TLT −11.6%) as the 2020–2024 rate-hike drawdown drags both averages up — but TLT's worsening is more than 2.5× IEF's.

Duration Mechanics

IEF — ~7.5yr Modified Duration
100 bp parallel rate rise → approx. −7.5% price loss. Recoverable within 1–2 years of coupon income (~4–5%), so a moderate rate rise need not produce a negative total return.
TLT — ~16–18yr Modified Duration
The same 100 bp rise wipes ~−17% from price. In 2022 the Fed hiked +425 bp in 12 months — TLT's worst year on record (−31%), erasing over a decade of coupon income in a single year.
